Exercise and The effects on The Mind and Body

By Lyndon Lewis

Why Exercise?

Truth is the best way to keep your body and mind in tip top shape is to be physically active. No matter what his or her physical condition, almost everyone can engage in at least some form of bodily exercise. To be at your best, you should follow the guidelines for your age and overall health status. However, each and every one of us can certainly exercise our minds even if our bodies don't always cooperate.

Reduces blood pressure

The number one cause of heart disease is Chronic Hypertension. Some causes of Hypertension include the increased plaque in the arteries that builds up from consuming a high-fat diet. Exercise helps to reduce your blood pressure, in part, by attacking plaque in your arteries. As the arteries widen, the blood flows through more freely, and your blood pressure eventually starts to drop. Hypertension also decreases as the result of exercise because your heart, a muscle, is getting a workout. The stronger your heart muscle gets, the greater it's ability to pump blood through the arteries, which also helps to reduce your blood pressure.

Know Where You Stand

As you probably already know, Type 2 diabetes is becoming a world-wide public health crisis. Even if you don't care about the health of the world, you should care about your own risk of diabetes.
